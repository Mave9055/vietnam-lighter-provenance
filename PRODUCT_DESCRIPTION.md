## Historical Significance

### The Hughes TH-55A Osage

In 1965, the U.S. Army adopted the **Hughes TH-55A "Osage"** as the primary trainer for Army Aviation. This revolutionary helicopter represented a major milestone in military aviation training, replacing older aircraft and establishing Fort Wolters, Texas, as the premier helicopter training facility for the U.S. Army.

This lighter commemorates the **first graduating class to train exclusively on the Hughes TH-55A**, making it a historically important marker of this transition in military aviation history.

### Recipient: WOC Arthur P. Lancy Jr.

**Service Record Correlation:** Official 25th Infantry Division "Tropic Lightning News" records from April 3, 1967, document that **WO Arthur P. Lancy, Co. B, 25th Avn. Bn.** was awarded the **Air Medal** for heroic achievement in aerial flight. This service timeline correlates precisely with a 1965 flight school graduation, establishing the authenticity of this artifact and the recipient's distinguished military service during the Vietnam War era.

**Archival Source:** [25th Infantry Division Association – Tropic Lightning News (April 3, 1967)](https://www.25thida.org/TLN/tln2-13.htm)
